Anik Saha

PhD Student. Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.

I am a fourth-year PhD student in Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. I am interested in Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning. My research is guided by Professor Bülent Yener and Professor Alex Gittens in the Computer Science department.

Research Overview
I have worked on multi-sense embeddings to address the meaning conflation problem in word embeddings that encode different meanings of a word into a single vector. The context is important to discern the correct sense of a word. So, we use pre-trained language models to associate the sense embeddings with the approapriate meaning. I have also explored the application of simple word embeddings in semantic search. During my internship at IBM last summer, I worked on information extraction from structured documents such as scanned images, pdfs etc.

Before starting my PhD at RPI, I graduated from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology in 2015 with a B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering. I was a Lecturer at Daffodil International University for one semester before joining Semion Inc. in 2016. There I worked on Natural Language Processing specifically sentiment analysis using deep learning.
